MATH COURSES

Overview and Goal

The mathematics department strives to empower students to interact with mathematics so they can experience the power and beauty of the subject. LWHS teaches students to approach math as a way of thinking, with the focus on inquiry, sense making, reasoning, and application. In each class, students have the opportunity to collaborate, persist, and seek multiple approaches in order to become more flexible and strategic problem solvers. Students develop a learning mindset by reflecting on mistakes and viewing them as opportunities for growth. With this mindset, students know that they can learn any mathematics they may need to achieve their goals in life.

Algebra 1

While many incoming 9th graders have taken a beginning algebra course in middle school, many students need to broaden and deepen their understanding of elementary algebra in order to have the tools to be successful in the courses that follow. Topics include: ratios, simplification and factoring of various kinds of variable expressions, solutions to linear/quadratic equations and linear inequalities/systems, graphing of two variable linear equations and inequalities, and mathematical modeling of real world situations. The course also introduces mathematical thinking skills that enable students to develop mastery based on reasoning and understanding of concepts, rather than memorization of rules and formulas.

Students who successfully complete this course move on to Inductive Geometry or Deductive Geometry with department recommendation.

This is a year-long course.

Inductive Geometry

The core focus of this course is to develop inductive and deductive reasoning skills. Students develop these skills by investigating geometric properties of lines, angles, similar and congruent polygons, circles, transformations, right triangle trigonometry, area, and volume. Students explore these properties through collaborative coursework and use of tools such as the compass, straightedge, and technology. Through these explorations, students develop ways to justify geometric properties. Students will become more confident and effective problem solvers through the practice of the schoolwide habits of mind and mathematical thinking skills. An extensive review of Algebra 1 topics is also integrated throughout the curriculum.

Students who successfully complete this course move on to Algebra 2 or Algebra 2 Accelerated with department recommendation.

Prerequisite: Algebra 1 at LWHS or department recommendation.

This is a year long course.

Deductive Geometry

This course is a more traditional geometry course, following the deductive approach and formal study of geometric concepts pioneered by Euclid. It benefits students who enjoy mathematical reasoning and have demonstrated a deep understanding of elementary algebra. Beginning with an introduction to logic, students prove the classical theorems of Euclidean Geometry and apply these ideas to challenging problems. Computers and manipulatives are used to explore and demonstrate geometric concepts, constructions, and transformations. Other topics include right triangle trigonometry and a brief overview of non Euclidean Geometry.

Students who successfully complete this course move on to Algebra 2 or Algebra 2 Accelerated with department recommendation.

Prerequisites: Algebra 1 and department recommendation.

This is a year-long course.

Algebra 2

Algebra 2 deepens and expands understanding of topics in elementary algebra, and introduces advanced topics that include: function notation, function transformations, arithmetic and geometric sequences, polynomial, exponential and logarithmic functions, inverse functions, and an introduction to complex numbers. A variety of approaches to problem solving are encouraged, with emphasis on multiple representations and mathematical thinking strategies. In addition to quizzes and cumulative tests, assessment may include write ups of problem solutions or concept explanations, formal oral presentations where students share mathematical reasoning of solutions. The course is centered on student investigation and discovery and gradual development of mastery, using materials written specifically for this class.

Students who successfully complete this course move on to Precalculus or Statistics.

Prerequisites: Algebra 1 and Inductive or Deductive Geometry.

This is a year-long course.

Algebra 2 Accelerated

Algebra 2 Accelerated is an intermediate algebra course intended for those who can benefit from a faster pace and a more theoretical approach. In addition to the material covered in Algebra 2, it includes a thorough treatment of complex numbers and polynomial/rational functions. It also adds topics such as matrices, conic sections, combinatorics, and the binomial theorem. The course encourages a variety of approaches to problem solving using mathematical models, with extensive use of graphical methods and graphing calculators.

Students who successfully complete this course move on to Precalculus or Honors Precalculus with department recommendation.

Prerequisites: Inductive or Deductive Geometry and department recommendation.

This is a year-long course.

Sample Syllabus | Allen & Chen

Precalculus

Precalculus focuses primarily on the study of polynomial, rational, exponential, logarithmic, and trigonometric functions. Through multiple perspectives, students explore transformations of functions, use functions as models of the physical world, and analyze characteristic properties.

Students who successfully complete this course move on to Calculus or Statistics.

Prerequisite: Algebra 2 with a second semester grade of at least a B or completion of Algebra 2 Accelerated.

This is a year-long course.

Honors Precalculus

This rigorous course builds upon and enriches content taught in Algebra 2 Accelerated, and covers the topics of Precalculus in greater depth and at a faster pace. Topics, in addition to those in Precalculus, may include conic sections, parametric equations, trigonometric forms of complex numbers, polar coordinates, iterations, fractals, and use of matrices for transformations of plane figures. Continuous review and problem solving are at the heart of the course, helping students to make connections, integrating previous and new concepts and skills.

Students who successfully complete this course move on to Calculus or Statistics.

Prerequisites: Algebra 2 (regular or accelerated) and department recommendation. Students coming from Algebra 2 will need to do additional study during the preceding summer and take a LWHS placement exam to determine final placement.

This is a year-long course.

Calculus

This course explores the fundamental concepts and techniques of differential and integral calculus—the mathematics of change. The emphasis is on conceptual understanding achieved through investigations, problem solving, and projects that require applications of calculus to mathematical modeling.

Prerequisite: Precalculus with semester grades of at least a B.

2. To enrich the math experience of a local elementary school.

Honors Calculus

This fast-paced and demanding class is equivalent to two semesters of college calculus. The course is structured around big ideas corresponding to the foundational concepts of Calculus: limits, derivatives, integrals and the fundamental theorem of calculus, and series. Students will take a deeper and more formal approach to these concepts and will solve problems by reasoning with definitions and theorems, implementing algebraic/computational processes, connecting multiple representations, and building notational fluency.

Prerequisites: Honors Precalculus and department recommendation. Students coming from Precalculus will need to do additional study during the preceding summer and take a LWHS placement exam to determine final placement.

This is a year-long course.

Honors Statistics 1: Descriptive Statistics and Data Visualizations PPP

Statistics is essential for any field in which people wish to draw meaningful conclusions from data. The goal is for students to become ethical and competent consumers and producers of statistics. The course has three major sections: data collection, descriptive statistics, and data visualization. Students will learn how to use different computer programs to create surveys, infographics, and interactive dashboards. Students will apply their knowledge and develop statistical skills to service projects within LWHS. Past projects include drug and alcohol presentations for the school, a course participation inventory for faculty, and microaggressions study for faculty and staff.

Prerequisites: Algebra 2 and department recommendation. This course is a prerequisite for Inferential Statistics in the spring.

This semester-long course offered in Fall 2017 fulfills the Public Purpose Program requirement for juniors and seniors.

Honors statistics 2: Inferential Statistics PPP

This course will answer that question, “When are we ever going to use this?” Topics focus on probability, personal finance, applied statistics, and basic economics. Students will investigate fundamentals of probability and combinatorics, culminating in the study of the gaming industry. Using spreadsheets, students will learn how interest is calculated with credit cards, how mortgages work, and how to save money through an annuity. Students will learn how polling and statistical values are reported in the media and the significance of margins of error. They will also learn basic visual coding in order to run simulations and to understand the predictive statistics that we see in our daily lives. A study of basic economic theory includes the study of supply and demand curves, elasticity, and other microeconomic topics.

Prerequisites: Algebra 2

This semester-long course is offered in the Spring.
